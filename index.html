Erin Anderson - BA Fine Art: Computational Arts

This portfolio site has been developed to accompany my application to the CCI Diploma in Creative Computing. The projects that I have compiled showcase the variety of work produced during my time studying towards my BA in Fine Art: Computational Arts.

New Terrain

New Terrain was an early project developed in Blender, exploring procedural landscapes and terrain generation. The project involved the use of noise displacement, experimental shaders and the application of geometry nodes.

Enter

A Beautiful Dream

A Beautiful Dream showcases my first exploration into developing my own custom AI image generation tool. I began this process by compiling a dataset of deepsea creatures, which were then used to train an image generation model on Runway ML. The results were then further refined using OpenAI's DALL·E 2.

Enter

Breaking the Machine

Breaking the Machine was conceptual and investigative exercise set in year one with the aim of breaking and glitching interfaces and tools within digital space. My exploration began with accessing the raw data of audio files and utilising this within AI tools such as Stable Diffusion and Notion's AI Assistant. My exploration moved towards translation and deciphering text with the application of various AI tools. Additionally, I overloaded Blender with broken geometry, and then went on to photocopy moving image.

Enter

Differential Mesh Growth

Differential Mesh Growth was an exploration into generative algorithms inspired by natural growth systems such as lichens, algae and coral. The results shown here were created using Boris Okunskiy's differential growth add on for Blender and explore various parametric variations and the skeletonisation of final forms.

Enter

Differential Line Growth

Continuing from Differential Mesh Growth, my exploration into simulating natural growth systems expanded with the use of Houdini. These pieces utilise an altered differential growth algorithm to generate lines, rather than mesh growth. Further examples within this projects subpage illustrate additional experiments within Blender, exploring instancing on points and noise displacement.

Enter

Diffusion Limited Aggregation

Diffusion Limited aggregation followed my prior explorations into natural growth systems through differential growth algorithms. Created in Houdini, this series illustrates otherworldly environments comprised of millions of clustered particles that have aggregated upon following a random walk algorithm.

Enter

Sanctuary

Sanctuary is an ongoing world-building project driven by the concept of meditative practice. Created in Blender, the process utilises the practice of digital kitbashing to curate natural scenes before using OpenAI's DALL·E 2 image variation tool to generate new variants.

Enter

Post Human Pavilion

Emanating from my Sanctuary series, Post Human Pavilion was developed as a speculative project proposal prompting a research lead investigation into regenerative practice and innovative digital fabrication techniques. Taking inspiration from Gilles Clément's Third Landscape, the project utilises a post human design ethos and utilitarian approach to the development of living sculptures.
